Step 3 of 7

How would you like to change your launch?

Higher launch maximizes carry and stopping power — ideal for most golfers, though more affected by wind. Lower launch performs better into the wind and maximizes roll-out.

Step 4 of 7

More spin, less spin, or similar?

More spin increases workability and stopping power, tightens front-to-back dispersion — at the cost of some distance and potentially more side-to-side movement. Less spin can reduce shot shape and maximize carry. No ball can fix a hook or slice on its own.

Step 5 of 7

Is feel important to you?

Softer balls are typically lower compression, which can trade some distance for a different feel at impact. Firmer balls tend to be higher-spinning models. Note: matching compression to swing speed is a common myth.

Step 6 of 7

How important is greenside performance?

Prioritizing more greenside spin results in a ball that spins more throughout the bag — great for control, but can affect distance and dispersion off the tee.
