Step 3 of 7

How would you like to change your launch?

Higher launch maximizes carry and stopping power — great for most golfers, though more wind-affected. Lower launch performs better into the wind and maximizes roll-out, but stopping power must come from spin instead.

Step 4 of 7

More spin, less spin, or similar?

More spin increases workability and stopping power — at the cost of some distance and potentially more side movement. Less spin can reduce shot shape and maximize carry — at the cost of stopping power.

Note: No ball can cure a hook or slice on its own.
Step 5 of 7

Is feel important to you?

Softer feeling balls are typically lower compression which can sacrifice distance. Firmer balls tend to be brands' higher-spinning models, but there are exceptions in both directions.

Step 6 of 7

How important is greenside performance?

Prioritizing more greenside spin generally results in a ball that spins more throughout the bag — good for control, but can affect distance and tee-to-green dispersion.
